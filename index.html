        Eating habits and strategy

        Eat for muscle, steady fat loss, energy, and a schedule you can actually maintain.

        No crash diets
        Build Every Meal

        Start with a palm-sized protein, add vegetables or fruit, choose a controlled serving of rice, potatoes, oats, or bread, and drink water.

        Protein Rule

        Include eggs, chicken, fish, lean meat, Greek yogurt, beans, lentils, or another protein source at every main meal.

        Busy-Day Defense

        Carry water and a planned snack such as fruit, yogurt, nuts, or a protein shake so City Tech, commuting, and delivery work do not force random choices.

        Uber Eats Rule

        Do not turn every pickup into a food decision. Eat a planned meal first, avoid liquid calories, and use a protein-plus-vegetable option when buying food.

        Sunday Preparation

        Prepare two proteins, one carb base, vegetables, fruit, and grab-and-go snacks. Repeat simple meals instead of depending on willpower.

        Recovery Nutrition

        Hydrate through the day, eat after training, include healthy fats, and protect 7-8 hours of sleep to support recovery and normal hormone health.

        Meal Timing

        Eat breakfast or a protein-rich first meal within a few hours of waking. Plan lunch before long classes or delivery work, eat a light protein-and-carb meal 1-3 hours before training, and eat protein with a normal meal after training.

        Simple Portion Guide

        At a main meal use 1-2 palms of protein, 1 fist of vegetables, 1 cupped hand of carbohydrates, and 1 thumb of healthy fat. Start there and adjust gradually from hunger, energy, body-weight trend, and training performance.

        If You Miss a Meal

        Do not binge or double every portion. Eat the next balanced meal when practical. If training is soon, use a quick option such as yogurt and fruit, a protein shake with a banana, eggs and toast, or chicken and rice. Return to the normal schedule afterward.

        80% planned food Protein each meal Water first Slow, sustainable progress

        Understand the system

        How food supports your training

        Train, recover, repeat
        1Protein builds

        Use protein at every main meal to repair muscle after Push, Pull, Legs, Upper, and Lower sessions.

        2Carbs fuel

        Place most rice, oats, potatoes, fruit, or bread before and after harder lifting sessions.

        3Fats support

        Use eggs, nuts, olive oil, avocado, and fish in controlled portions for health and steady energy.

        4Water performs

        Drink through the day and add extra water around training, delivery work, heat, and long commutes.

        5Recovery adapts

        Rest days still need protein. Reduce portions slightly if activity is lower, but do not starve recovery.

        Morning to night, matched with training

        Adjust portions to hunger and progress

        This is a general planning guide, not medical nutrition advice. Keep protein consistent, use sensible portions, and adjust for allergies, medical needs, and your actual calorie requirements.

        Daily Ramadan Rhythm

        1. Wake for suhoor, hydrate, make intention, and stop eating before Fajr.
        2. Pray all five prayers on time and protect Fajr and Isha especially.
        3. Read Quran daily with English understanding and one written action.
        4. Guard speech, eyes, anger, scrolling, and unnecessary arguments.
        5. Break the fast at Maghrib, make dua, pray, eat with control, and avoid waste.
        6. Pray Taraweeh or additional night prayer according to your ability.

        Last Ten Nights

        1. Increase Quran, dua, charity, dhikr, repentance, and night prayer.
        2. Seek Laylat al-Qadr throughout the odd nights.
        3. Reduce entertainment and protect the nights from wasted time.
        4. Ask Allah for forgiveness, guidance, discipline, health, family, and a strong future.
